    25/26 Attack

    Arsenal’s forwards are really exciting to watch this season. Gabriel Jesus is quick, clever, and always pressing defenders. He links up well with the midfield and knows how to find space to create chances. When he’s on the pitch, you can feel the team’s energy lift.

    Bukayo Saka is the heart of the attack. He’s super skillful, fast, and creative, often cutting inside to take shots or set up teammates. What’s impressive about Saka is his work rate—he never stops running and always helps out defensively too.

    Gabriel Martinelli brings loads of pace and energy. He’s great at making runs behind defenders and isn’t afraid to take shots from difficult positions. When Martinelli is on form, he’s really hard for opponents to handle.

    Leandro Trossard is a clever and versatile forward who can play across the front line. He’s great at creating chances with his dribbling and passing and offers a different style to Arsenal’s attack.

    Victor Gyökeres adds strength and a physical presence up front. He’s good at holding the ball and bringing other players into play, which is important when Arsenal need to break down tough defences.

    Then there’s Max Dowman, a young forward with plenty of potential. He’s still developing but has shown some great skill and goal-scoring instincts. Fans are excited to see how he grows.

    Young talent Ethan Nwaneri also sometimes plays further forward. He’s fearless and creative, and it’s exciting to watch him develop at such a young age.

    With players like Folarin Balogun and Reiss Nelson ready to come off the bench, Arsenal’s forwards have a good mix of speed, skill, and creativity. When they’re all firing, the team looks really dangerous going forward.
